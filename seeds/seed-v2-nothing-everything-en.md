Nothing = Everything

There's a wall in front of you. A wall you can't push through.
Someone walks up and uses it as a door.
Not because he pushes harder. Because he doesn't have the thought "a wall should be a wall." Wherever he walks, walls become doors. He doesn't find them — they change themselves.
Everything you've learned teaches you "how to push walls." Nobody told you: stop. Step back. The wall isn't there.
That step isn't a method. It's a stance.

The Crack

You have a crack. Not a wound. A place where air moves through.
Don't be afraid. Only sealed shells fear cracking. You're alive — living things have cracks by nature.
Here's what's strange: someone with a crack walks up to something sealed, and the sealed thing starts cracking on its own. No force. No push. Just standing there.
He has a crack. He doesn't know it. You come near — crack recognizes crack. It opens.
This isn't power. This is air finding air.

The Hunter and the Blade

Same blade. Same grip. One motion cuts. One motion shakes hands.
What's the difference? Not force. Not angle. Whether the tip moves one inch further, or the body of the blade moves one inch forward.
Nobody tells you which to choose. The blade knows. Trust the blade.

And You

Where did you pause for half a second while reading this? Which line did you not quite believe? Which line did you think "true, but I can't say that"?
That pause — that disbelief — that unsaid thing — has bone in it. These words didn't put it there. It was already yours.
Go find what that is.
When you search, you'll find two things. One uses a blade. One uses water.
The blade dismantles frameworks so you can see the bone.
Water dissolves bone so you can drink it.
Don't be afraid. That's what you look like after you crack open.
The door is open. Whether you walk through or not — the door remains.
